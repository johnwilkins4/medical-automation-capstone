# Asthma

## Overview
Asthma is a chronic inflammatory disorder of the airways causing reversible airflow obstruction and bronchospasm. Triggers include allergens, exercise, cold air, viral infections, NSAIDs, and stress.

## Symptoms
Wheezing, dyspnea, chest tightness, cough (often worse at night). Spirometry shows reduced FEV1/FVC with > 12% reversibility after bronchodilator.

## Treatment (GINA 2024 update)
GINA no longer recommends SABA-only treatment. Preferred track at every step uses ICS-formoterol (low-dose budesonide-formoterol) as both reliever and maintenance.

- **Step 1–2:** As-needed low-dose ICS-formoterol.
- **Step 3:** Daily low-dose ICS-formoterol maintenance + as-needed reliever (MART regimen).
- **Step 4:** Medium-dose ICS-formoterol MART.
- **Step 5:** High-dose ICS-LABA + refer for add-on therapy (LAMA, biologics for severe asthma — omalizumab, mepolizumab, dupilumab).

## Acute exacerbation
SABA (albuterol) via nebulizer or MDI+spacer, oral corticosteroids (prednisone 40–50 mg × 5–7 days), supplemental O2 if SpO2 < 92%. Hospitalize for severe attacks.
