# Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)

## Overview
Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) is a gradual loss of kidney function over months to years. The kidneys filter wastes and excess fluids from the blood; as function declines, wastes accumulate and can cause other health problems including cardiovascular disease, anemia, and bone disease. CKD is staged from 1 (mildest) to 5 (kidney failure) based on the estimated glomerular filtration rate (eGFR) and the presence of kidney damage markers such as albuminuria.

## Causes and risk factors
The two leading causes of CKD worldwide are diabetes mellitus and hypertension. Other causes include glomerulonephritis, polycystic kidney disease, obstructive uropathy, and prolonged use of nephrotoxic medications (e.g., NSAIDs). Risk factors include age over 60, family history of kidney disease, and cardiovascular disease.

## Symptoms
Early CKD is often asymptomatic and is usually detected on routine labs. Later stages can present with fatigue, decreased appetite, muscle cramps, swelling in the feet or ankles, difficulty concentrating, and changes in urination.

## Diagnosis
Diagnosis is based on eGFR (calculated from serum creatinine) and urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio (UACR). Imaging (renal ultrasound) and kidney biopsy may be used to determine underlying cause.

## Treatment (current standard of care as of 2024-2025)
Treatment aims to slow progression and manage complications.

1. **Blood pressure control.** ACE inhibitors (e.g., enalapril) or angiotensin II receptor blockers (e.g., losartan) are first-line; they reduce proteinuria and slow progression.
2. **Glycemic control** in diabetic CKD. HbA1c target individualized (usually ~7.0%).
3. **SGLT2 inhibitors** (empagliflozin, dapagliflozin) — now considered standard for CKD with or without diabetes; landmark trials (DAPA-CKD, EMPA-KIDNEY) showed reduced progression and cardiovascular mortality.
4. **Non-steroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonists** (finerenone) — approved for diabetic CKD; FIDELIO-DKD / FIGARO-DKD trials demonstrated renal and cardiovascular benefit.
5. **GLP-1 receptor agonists** (semaglutide) — emerging evidence (FLOW trial, 2024) of renal benefit in diabetic CKD.
6. **Dietary modification.** Sodium restriction (<2 g/day), moderate protein restriction in stages 3–4, potassium and phosphorus restriction as indicated.
7. **Statins** for cardiovascular risk reduction.
8. **Treatment of anemia** (iron, ESAs) and mineral bone disorder (phosphate binders, vitamin D analogs).
9. **Renal replacement therapy** (dialysis or transplant) for stage 5 / ESRD.

## Monitoring
Regular monitoring of eGFR, UACR, blood pressure, potassium, phosphorus, calcium, PTH, and hemoglobin.

## Patient education
- Avoid nephrotoxic drugs (NSAIDs, certain antibiotics without dose adjustment, contrast without hydration).
- Hydration, smoking cessation, weight management.
- Annual flu and pneumococcal vaccinations.
