# COVID-19 (SARS-CoV-2 infection)

## Overview
COVID-19 is caused by SARS-CoV-2. Most cases are mild; risk factors for severe disease include age > 65, immunocompromise, obesity, diabetes, CKD, cardiovascular disease.

## Symptoms
Fever, cough, sore throat, fatigue, headache, loss of taste/smell (less common with current variants), myalgia, GI symptoms. Severe disease: dyspnea, hypoxia.

## Diagnosis
Rapid antigen test (home or POC) or RT-PCR.

## Outpatient treatment (2024–2025 guidance)
For non-hospitalized patients at high risk of progression, begin within 5 days of symptom onset:
- **Nirmatrelvir-ritonavir (Paxlovid)** — oral 5-day course, preferred first-line.
- **Remdesivir** — 3-day IV course if Paxlovid contraindicated (drug interactions, severe renal impairment).
- **Molnupiravir** — oral, less effective; use when others unavailable.

Symptomatic care: acetaminophen, hydration, rest.

## Hospitalized patients
Dexamethasone for those on supplemental O2. Remdesivir for non-intubated. Tocilizumab or baricitinib for severe inflammation. Anticoagulation per protocol.

## Vaccination
Stay up to date with the current seasonal formulation. Especially important for adults ≥ 65, immunocompromised, and pregnant persons.

## Long COVID
Persistent symptoms > 12 weeks after infection: fatigue, cognitive dysfunction, dyspnea, POTS-like symptoms. Management is symptomatic and multidisciplinary.
