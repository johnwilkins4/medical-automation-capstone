# Depression and Anxiety (Primary Care Overview)

## Major Depressive Disorder (MDD)
Diagnosis: ≥ 5 of 9 SIGECAPS symptoms for ≥ 2 weeks, one of which is depressed mood or anhedonia. Screen with PHQ-9.

### Treatment
- Mild: psychotherapy (CBT, IPT) alone is reasonable.
- Moderate–severe: combine pharmacotherapy + psychotherapy.
- First-line meds: SSRIs (sertraline, escitalopram, fluoxetine) or SNRIs (venlafaxine, duloxetine).
- Second-line: bupropion, mirtazapine.
- Expect 4–6 weeks for full effect; titrate before switching.
- Treatment-resistant: augment with bupropion, lithium, atypical antipsychotic, or ketamine/esketamine; consider ECT for severe / psychotic / catatonic depression.

## Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)
Diagnosis: excessive worry ≥ 6 months + 3 of 6 somatic symptoms. Screen with GAD-7.

### Treatment
- First-line: SSRIs or SNRIs; CBT is equally effective.
- Buspirone as adjunct.
- Benzodiazepines reserved for short-term use due to dependence risk.

## Safety
Screen for suicidal ideation at every visit. High-risk: prior attempt, hopelessness, recent major stressor.
