# Heart Failure

## Overview
Heart failure (HF) is a clinical syndrome in which the heart cannot pump enough blood to meet the body's needs. Classified by left ventricular ejection fraction: HFrEF (≤ 40%), HFmrEF (41–49%), HFpEF (≥ 50%).

## Symptoms
Dyspnea on exertion, orthopnea, paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea, peripheral edema, fatigue, reduced exercise tolerance.

## Diagnosis
Clinical + echocardiogram + BNP/NT-proBNP. Rule out precipitants (ischemia, arrhythmia, valve disease, infection, medication non-adherence).

## Treatment — HFrEF (the "Four Pillars" per ACC/AHA/HFSA 2022, still standard 2025)
1. **ARNI** (sacubitril-valsartan) — preferred over ACEi/ARB unless contraindicated.
2. **Beta-blocker** — carvedilol, metoprolol succinate, or bisoprolol.
3. **MRA** — spironolactone or eplerenone.
4. **SGLT2 inhibitor** — dapagliflozin or empagliflozin; now first-line regardless of diabetes status.

Add loop diuretic (furosemide) for congestion. Device therapy (ICD, CRT) for select patients.

## Treatment — HFpEF
SGLT2 inhibitors are the first therapy with a positive mortality signal. Treat comorbidities aggressively (HTN, AFib, obesity).

## Patient self-management
Daily weights, salt restriction (< 2 g/day), fluid restriction if advised, medication adherence, flu vaccination.
