# Essential Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)

## Overview
Essential (primary) hypertension is persistently elevated blood pressure without an identifiable secondary cause. It is the leading modifiable risk factor for cardiovascular disease, stroke, and chronic kidney disease globally.

## Classification (ACC/AHA 2017, reaffirmed 2024)
- Normal: < 120/80
- Elevated: 120–129 / < 80
- Stage 1: 130–139 / 80–89
- Stage 2: ≥ 140 / ≥ 90

## Diagnosis
Confirmed on two or more occasions, ideally with out-of-office measurements (home or ambulatory BP monitoring) to rule out white-coat effect.

## Treatment

### Lifestyle (first-line for all patients)
- **DASH diet** (rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, low-fat dairy).
- Sodium < 2300 mg/day (< 1500 mg/day preferred).
- Weight loss — ~1 mmHg drop per kg lost.
- Physical activity: 150 min/week moderate aerobic.
- Limit alcohol: ≤ 2 drinks/day (men), ≤ 1/day (women).
- Smoking cessation.

### Pharmacotherapy
Start in most Stage 1 patients with ASCVD risk ≥ 10% or comorbidities (diabetes, CKD), and in all Stage 2 patients.

**First-line drug classes:**
1. Thiazide diuretics (chlorthalidone, hydrochlorothiazide).
2. ACE inhibitors (lisinopril, enalapril).
3. ARBs (losartan, telmisartan) — preferred over ACEi if cough develops.
4. Calcium channel blockers (amlodipine).

Start with single agent; many patients require 2+ drugs — combination pills (e.g., ARB + thiazide) improve adherence.

### Special populations
- **Black adults without CKD or HF:** CCB or thiazide preferred over ACEi/ARB as initial monotherapy.
- **CKD or diabetes:** ACEi or ARB preferred for renal protection.
- **Heart failure:** ACEi/ARB + beta-blocker + diuretic.
- **Pregnancy:** labetalol, nifedipine, methyldopa; ACEi/ARBs contraindicated.

## Target BP
Generally < 130/80 mmHg. In adults ≥ 65 without substantial comorbidity, < 130/80 is reasonable. More lenient targets for frail elderly.

## Monitoring
Home BP monitoring encouraged. Recheck uncontrolled HTN monthly until controlled, then every 3–6 months.
