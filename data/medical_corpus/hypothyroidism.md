# Hypothyroidism

## Overview
Hypothyroidism is under-production of thyroid hormone. Primary hypothyroidism (thyroid gland failure) accounts for > 95% of cases; most commonly due to Hashimoto's thyroiditis or post-ablative causes.

## Symptoms
Fatigue, weight gain, cold intolerance, constipation, dry skin, hair loss, menstrual irregularity, bradycardia, delayed reflexes, depression.

## Diagnosis
- Elevated TSH with low free T4 = overt hypothyroidism.
- Elevated TSH with normal free T4 = subclinical hypothyroidism.
- Check anti-TPO antibodies to confirm Hashimoto's.

## Treatment
**Levothyroxine (T4)** is the standard. Start 1.6 mcg/kg/day in healthy adults; lower (25–50 mcg) in elderly or those with cardiac disease. Take on empty stomach, 30–60 min before food.

**Recheck TSH** 6–8 weeks after initiation or dose change. Target TSH usually 0.5–2.5 mIU/L for most; elderly may be up to 4–6.

Combination T4/T3 therapy is not routinely recommended but may be considered for persistent symptoms despite normal TSH on T4 monotherapy.
