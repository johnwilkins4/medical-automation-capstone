# Osteoarthritis (OA)

## Overview
Osteoarthritis is progressive degeneration of articular cartilage and subchondral bone. Most commonly affects knees, hips, hands, and spine. Risk factors: age, obesity, joint injury, genetics.

## Symptoms
Joint pain worse with activity, stiffness < 30 min after rest, crepitus, functional limitation.

## Diagnosis
Clinical; X-ray shows joint-space narrowing, osteophytes, subchondral sclerosis. Labs normal (unlike inflammatory arthritis).

## Treatment
### Non-pharmacologic (foundation)
Weight loss (5–10% reduction meaningfully reduces knee OA pain), exercise (aerobic + strengthening), physical therapy, assistive devices.

### Pharmacologic
- Topical NSAIDs (diclofenac gel) — first-line for knee/hand OA.
- Oral NSAIDs — short-term; caution in elderly, CKD, CV disease.
- Acetaminophen — modest efficacy.
- Intra-articular corticosteroid injections — short-term relief.
- Duloxetine — for chronic knee OA with centralized pain.

### Surgical
Joint replacement (TKA, THA) for advanced disease refractory to conservative management.

### Not recommended
Glucosamine/chondroitin lack consistent evidence. Hyaluronic acid injections — 2019 ACR/AF guideline conditionally against for knee OA.
