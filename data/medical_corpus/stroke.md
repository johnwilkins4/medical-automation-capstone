# Stroke (Ischemic and Hemorrhagic)

## Overview
Stroke is a sudden neurologic deficit due to cerebrovascular cause. 85% ischemic, 15% hemorrhagic. "Time is brain": every minute of large-vessel ischemia destroys ~1.9 million neurons.

## Symptoms (BE-FAST)
Balance loss, Eyes (vision changes), Face droop, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty, Time to call 911.

## Acute treatment — ischemic
- **IV thrombolysis (alteplase or tenecteplase)** within 4.5 hours of onset for eligible patients.
- **Mechanical thrombectomy** within 24 hours for large-vessel occlusion meeting imaging criteria.

## Acute treatment — hemorrhagic
Rapid BP control (target SBP < 140), reverse anticoagulation, neurosurgery consultation.

## Secondary prevention
Antiplatelet therapy (aspirin, clopidogrel, or dual antiplatelet short term), statin, blood pressure control, diabetes management, anticoagulation for atrial fibrillation (DOAC preferred), smoking cessation, carotid intervention if significant stenosis.
