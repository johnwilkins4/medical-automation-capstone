# Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (T2DM)

## Overview
Type 2 Diabetes is a chronic metabolic disorder characterized by insulin resistance and progressive beta-cell dysfunction, leading to hyperglycemia. It accounts for ~90% of all diabetes cases and is strongly associated with obesity, sedentary lifestyle, and genetic predisposition.

## Symptoms
Polyuria, polydipsia, polyphagia, fatigue, blurred vision, slow wound healing, recurrent infections. Many patients are asymptomatic at diagnosis and are found via routine screening.

## Diagnosis (ADA criteria)
Any of: HbA1c ≥ 6.5%, fasting plasma glucose ≥ 126 mg/dL, 2-hr OGTT ≥ 200 mg/dL, or random glucose ≥ 200 mg/dL with symptoms.

## Treatment (current standards, ADA 2025)

### First-line
**Metformin** remains first-line pharmacotherapy unless contraindicated (eGFR < 30, active liver disease). Dose-titrate to 1000 mg BID with meals to minimize GI side effects.

### Second-line — patient-centered choice
Choice depends on comorbidities:
- **ASCVD or high cardiovascular risk** → GLP-1 receptor agonist (semaglutide, liraglutide) or SGLT2 inhibitor (empagliflozin, dapagliflozin) with proven CV benefit.
- **Heart failure or CKD** → SGLT2 inhibitor first.
- **Need for weight loss** → GLP-1 RA (semaglutide, tirzepatide).
- **Cost-sensitive** → sulfonylureas (glipizide, glimepiride) or thiazolidinedione (pioglitazone).

### Insulin
Added when HbA1c remains above target despite multiple oral/non-insulin injectable agents, or at diagnosis if HbA1c > 10% or symptomatic hyperglycemia.

## Targets
- HbA1c: typically <7% (individualized — looser for elderly or those with short life expectancy).
- Blood pressure: <130/80 mmHg.
- LDL-C: statin therapy for most adults with diabetes age 40–75.

## Monitoring
Quarterly HbA1c if not at target, then every 6 months. Annual: lipid panel, urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio, dilated eye exam, foot exam.

## Lifestyle
- Weight loss of 5–10% improves glycemic control substantially.
- 150 min/week moderate-intensity aerobic activity + 2–3 sessions resistance training.
- Mediterranean or DASH-style diet; carbohydrate counting for insulin users.

## Complications to screen for
Retinopathy, nephropathy, neuropathy, cardiovascular disease, diabetic foot, depression.
